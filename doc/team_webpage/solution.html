Solution

 

The application will be a web-based application, which will allow ease of access for students and professional bassoonists. The application will also be viewable on mobile devices (such as an iPhone or an Android). Users will be able to 'search' for fingerings by entering up to three notes on a graphic figure. The system will then display a possible fingering for that note (taking into consideration the note preceding and the note following the indicated note). If the user wants to see another fingering, they are able to click to the next fingering. To suggest fingerings, a heuristic will also be employed to determine the best match for a note in specific situations. The heuristic will take into account how good a note will sound with a particular fingering versus how 'far away' the fingering is. (Some situations may require a fingering that produces a note that doesn't sound perfect, but is much easier to play for fast passages, etc.) Users will also be able to 'like' or 'dislike' fingerings (e.g. perhaps a fingering is not used very often, or just isn't reasonable) and rate the difficulty of a fingering (e.g. perhaps a fingering is best used by professionals for a certain note). Users will be able to add fingerings to the database. Authorized users will be able to delete unwanted or 'spam' fingerings if necessary.
